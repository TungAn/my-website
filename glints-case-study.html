From Cold Application to Conversations

How a Chat-Driven Increased Glints Matching Efficiency

8 min read

Company

Glints

Role

Design Lead

Timeline

Now

I joined Glints in Q2 2022 to expand their existing mobile app into the Vietnam market. Shortly after, I took on a larger opportunity to lead a design transformation to redefine the job-matching experience for the overall Southeast Asia market.

By shifting from Resume-based applications to Chat-driven interactions, my design contributed to overall matching efficiency by 64%, driving a sustained 16% Month-over-Month growth.

This change also accelerated employer response times, nearly doubling the percentage of applications processed within a day from 9.7% to 18.4%. The initiative aligned with Glints' vision of a mobile-first strategy and an SME-friendly job platform.

The Challenge

When I joined Glints, the platform faced critical engagement and conversion challenges:

Mobile underperformance: Despite driving 2.3x more mobile traffic, mobile monthly active users (MAU) were half that of the desktop.

High application drop-offs: 80% of signed-up users didn't reach the application state on their 1st day, citing irrelevant job matches and friction in resume uploads.

Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) are underserved: Keyword-based job searches favor large enterprises, making it difficult for small and medium businesses to attract talent.

Slow hiring process: Resume-based applications led to sluggish employer response times, with only 20% of applications being processed, frustrating job seekers.

Glints needed a mobile-first, interactive hiring experience that improved job matching, reduced application friction, and accelerated employer response times.

My Role

During my time at Glints, I was responsible for:

  • Redesigning the job application experience by leading the end-to-end design of chat-based applications, replacing traditional Resume-based ones.
  • Optimizing the candidate experience to improve job matching efficiency, including data normalization for personalization and reducing friction in the application journey.
  • Building the foundation for a mobile-first employer app to increase adoption among existing web users and target specific mobile-user segments.
  • Improving the mobile app monetization strategy, contributing to ~15% of the platform's revenue weekly.

The Transformation Process

Start with an MVP

From strategic alignment, the leadership wanted to explore a new way to replace the current recruitment behavior, which relied heavily on resumes.

I led a comprehensive research initiative combining quantitative and qualitative methods to understand user behavior, pain points, and market trends.

The Human Story: Young Talent Struggling to Get Noticed

Through user interviews, I met Tuan Anh, a 22-year-old recent graduate in Ho Chi Minh City, who shared a common frustration:

"I've applied to dozens of jobs and mostly use my laptop due to its convenience. However, I rarely hear back. Employers view my resume, but nothing happens. I don't even know if my resume is good enough or if I'm missing something important."

- Tuan Anh

This lack of feedback and uncertainty was a recurring theme across interviews, mirroring inefficiencies in traditional job platforms.

From the Data: Identifying the Core Problem

  • 80% of signed-up users didn't apply on their 1st day, where 50% of them view the job details but drop off.
  • 20% of applications being processed, result is lacking response from employers.
  • Only 8% of new applicants return to apply again, demonstrating the low user engagement and negative overall experience.

Additionally, most job searches relied on keyword-based matching, favoring large enterprises with internal hiring systems. This made them less engaged with platforms and slower to respond to candidates.

In China, 51Job and Zhilian struggled with the same challenges due to a passive, one-sided job search experience.

Market Validation: Lessons from China's Hiring Evolution

In the 2010s, Boss Zhipin disrupted China's recruitment industry by replacing resume-based applications with real-time chat, making the hiring process faster and more engaging.

The market shifted to mobile-first job-seeking, with Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) driving demand. As a result:

  • Direct chat increased engagement, boosting daily app opens to 8.1x compared to 4-6x on traditional platforms.
  • SMEs rapidly adopted chat-based hiring, with 97% of Boss Zhipin's paying employers being small businesses.

These insights reinforced our strategy: A mobile-first, chat-driven hiring model could significantly boost engagement and response rates in Southeast Asia.

The Turning Point: Testing the Chat-Based Model

To test our innovative approach, I designed and collaborated with engineering teams to develop a minimum viable product (MVP) - a lightweight chat-based system integrated into the current system. We carefully rolled out this MVP to a specific user segment, meticulously measuring engagement and conversion rates.

The results were transformative:

  • Matching efficiency 2x compared to traditional application methods
  • Shortlisted applications increased from approximately 10% to 40%
  • User interviews revealed overwhelmingly positive feedback, with candidates appreciating the chat feature's seamless integration

Candidates specifically noted the chat interface as a safe, convenient communication channel that provides faster updates and more direct interaction with potential employers.

"I expect to use the chat to increase the chance to be seen by employers. So Increase the chance of getting hired"

- Quoc Viet

"I follow up on CV screening results after sending my CV (by email). And to know more about companies' culture, direction."

- Thao Linh

From the employer side, easily connecting to the candidate and tracking the candidate through historical chat are what employers expect.

"It helps me easily connect with candidates"

- Ms. Huyen, Education Builder

"Can see historical data to jog my memories on why or why not I pick this candidate so I can better track them"

- District Eight
